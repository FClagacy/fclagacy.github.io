Overview

The Argentina national football team, administered by the Argentine Football Association (AFA), represents Argentina in international men's football. Argentina is one of the most successful national teams in the world, having won three FIFA World Cups (1978, 1986, 2022) and a record-tying 15 Copa América titles.

Argentina is known for its passionate style of play, technical skill, and production of world-class players, including the legendary Diego Maradona and Lionel Messi, widely considered among the greatest footballers of all time.

Historical Legends

Argentina has produced some of the greatest footballers in history. Diego Maradona is widely regarded as one of the greatest players of all time, leading Argentina to World Cup victory in 1986. Lionel Messi, after winning multiple Ballon d'Or awards, finally led Argentina to World Cup glory in 2022. Other legends include:

  • Alfredo Di Stéfano - Considered one of the greatest players of all time, though he played most of his career for Spain
  • Mario Kempes - Top scorer and star of Argentina's 1978 World Cup winning team
  • Gabriel Batistuta - Argentina's all-time top scorer until surpassed by Messi
  • Juan Román Riquelme - Masterful playmaker known for his vision and technique
  • Javier Zanetti - Legendary defender and Argentina's most-capped outfield player
  • Sergio Agüero - Prolific striker and one of Argentina's all-time top scorers
